PhD Proposal

Quantifying Player Decision-Making Effectiveness

Developing a research framework that uses neural networks to evaluate the quality of decisions made by players within in-game situations — moving beyond outcome-based metrics (goals, assists) to assess whether a player's choice (passing, dribbling, shooting) was optimal given the spatial and tactical context. Draws on event and tracking data, contextual feature engineering, and sequence modelling to isolate decision quality from execution quality.

Directly applicable to scouting, tactical analysis, and player development — answering not just what happened, but was it the right call?
